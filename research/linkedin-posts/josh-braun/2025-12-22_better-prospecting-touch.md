# Josh Braun — Better prospecting touch

## Raw content

One of the biggest myths in prospecting is this:

“You need 14 touches in 25 days before someone will respond.”

So we build sequences like this.
Emails. 
Calls. 
DMs. 
Voice notes. 
Bumps.

An avalanche of activity that makes everyone feel a little gross.

Here’s the thing.

You don’t need more touches.
You need a better touch.

Most outreach is designed around what the seller wants.
A demo. A meeting. A chance to pitch.

But the buyer doesn’t want that.

They’re getting their job done.
They’re not shopping for something new. 
A pitch is like asking to meet someone’s parents on the first date.

Too much. Too soon.

The shift is simple:
Stop pitching. 
Start teaching.

Teach something useful about a topic your prospect cares about.
No ask. 
No pressure. 
No “Any thoughts?”

Here’s an example for my business:

“Hey Pete, I put together a short slideshow called
The Art of Cold Calling: Starting Conversations With People Who Weren’t Planning on Having One.

It’s a distillation of what I’ve learned from thousands of cold calls, training teams, and plenty of trial and error.

Want to check it out? 

If it sparks an “oh wow” moment and you want to chat great.

If not, feel free to delete it and pretend this never happened.”

That’s it.

Aligned with the buyer.
Useful.

When the touch aligns with the buyer, 
you don’t need 14 of them.
