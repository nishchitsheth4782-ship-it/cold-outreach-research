## Raw content

Here's a breakdown of a good cold email.

Blue. Relevance: It opens with something specific about the prospect (fleet size and telematics use). Answers the question, “Why are you emailing me?”

Green. Poke the bear question: “How are you using that data to make sure you’re not overpaying?” doesn’t push. It invites. It opens a loop.

Yellow. Knowledge gap: It introduces a potential blind spot (lack of tax granularity) the prospect might not be thinking about. It's specific or "crispy" which make it relatable. 

Orange. Social proof: 2,000+ fleets and a $10M result. Specific, credible, and not overhyped.

Pink. Preemptively addresses the objection: Speaks directly to the most common pushback: We’re already doing this. Instead of ignoring it, the line validates their setup and gently introduces doubt.

Grey. Low-pressure ask: “No rush” lowers defenses. It doesn’t beg. It respects their time and space.

Yes, you can steal the template, but it’s the underlying psychology that will help you apply it to your prospects.
